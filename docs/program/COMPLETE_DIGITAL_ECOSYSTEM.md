## 5. Architecture principles

- GitHub and Vercel remain authoritative for the customer website.
- Replit Command Center is a separate internal operations application, not a replacement website.
- Each business concept has one authoritative system of record.
- New modules are API-first, bilingual Arabic/English, mobile-ready, accessible, auditable, and versioned.
- Sample data, verified project data, customer data, learner data, health-related data, and credentials are visibly separated.
- Long-term features must not destabilize the current website or delay hard commercial gates without an evidence-backed reason.
- Build-versus-integrate choices consider security, reliability, portability, operating cost, data ownership, and vendor lock-in.

## 6. Agent operating model

Every workstream records:

- current task;
- confirmed progress and evidence;
- blockers;
- files or artifacts being changed;
- start and completion state;
- next isolated action;
- QA status;
- owner or qualified-professional decision needs.

An open GitHub Issue is an assignment, not proof that an autonomous agent is executing. Status becomes `IN_PROGRESS_EVIDENCED` only when an inspectable artifact, comment, PR, tool result, test, design, or report proves progress.

Completion notifications include an Arabic summary, deliverables, evidence, remaining risks, next action, and any unavoidable decision.

## 7. Quality Department

Nothing public or operationally sensitive is approved solely because it was generated or drafted.

Quality gates cover:

- design, identity, Arabic typography, dimensions, mobile presentation, rights, and export quality;
- video image, audio, duration, captions, rights, CTA, and mobile delivery;
- website speed, accessibility, SEO, conversion, UX, security, and evidence;
- content language, factual claims, attraction, conversion, rights, and channel fit;
- AI outputs, sources, dates, assumptions, limitations, bias, safety, coach review, and scope boundaries;
- learner/family reports, separation of facts and suggestions, privacy, sharing permissions, and plain language.

## 8. AI Swimming Education Assistant

The assistant is a coach-facing educational planning tool.

It may support:

- water-safety and learn-to-swim progressions;
- freestyle, backstroke, breaststroke, and butterfly skills;
- breathing, body position, rotation, propulsion, starts, and turns;
- drills, games, cues, equipment, easier/harder variations, success criteria, and session structures;
- parent-friendly educational summaries.

Every output includes inputs, assumptions, objectives, safety prerequisites, progression/regression, success and stop criteria, evidence references, review date, and coach approval.

It must not diagnose, treat, prescribe rehabilitation, guarantee outcomes, recommend unsupervised lessons, use forced submersion, or use unsafe breath-holding tasks.

## 9. Adaptive Aquatics Education Assistant

The adaptive module creates individualized swimming-teaching strategies from verified needs, preferences, family information, professional restrictions where supplied, and coach observations.

It may consider:

- communication method;
- sensory preferences and triggers;
- transitions, attention, choice, and reinforcement preferences;
- mobility and support needs;
- water confidence and current skills;
- learner/family goals;
- environmental adaptations;
- supervision and water-safety requirements.

A diagnosis label alone never determines the plan. The system does not promise improvement in senses, attention, behavior, development, pain, or medical function. It does not use forced eye contact, coercion, punishment, restraint, or forced submersion.

## 10. Aquatic exercise and rehabilitation boundary

The product follows three lanes:

### Lane A — Swimming education

Water safety, confidence, buoyancy, breath control, stroke teaching, games, and communication adaptations within verified instructor competence.

### Lane B — General aquatic exercise

Non-diagnostic fitness, mobility, balance, and conditioning ideas for appropriate participants, subject to screening, facility rules, participant comfort, and instructor competence.

### Lane C — Clinician-supervised rehabilitation

Condition-specific clinical assessment, treatment goals, post-operative care, pain/injury management, neurological rehabilitation, therapeutic handling, and clinical outcomes require an appropriately licensed professional. The platform may organize supplied professional instructions and observations but does not independently prescribe this lane.

Public titles and claims require separate factual and UAE regulatory verification.

## 11. Learner and family records

Before sensitive-data approval, designs use anonymous learner codes and fictional/sample records only.

Future records may include:

- educational assessment;
- water-safety and skill baseline;
- learner/family goals and preferences;
- coach observations and accommodations;
- lesson plans and completed activities;
- measurable educational progress;
- next-step decisions;
- family-facing educational reports.

Verified facts, parent statements, coach observations, professional instructions, and AI suggestions remain separate.

No identifiable child, disability, diagnosis, health, family, or safeguarding data is stored until Privacy/Consent, security, access, retention, vendor, correction, deletion, and sharing rules are approved.

## 12. Aquatics Evidence Center

Recommendations that claim to be current or condition-specific require:

- source and publisher;
- publication or update date;
- target population and setting;
- evidence type and strength;
- practical implication;
- limitations and contraindications;
- UAE applicability notes where relevant;
- next review date.

Official governing bodies, recognized qualification providers, professional associations, standards, primary research, and systematic reviews are prioritized. Coaching convention, professional consensus, and research evidence are labeled separately.

Proprietary course content is not copied without rights.

## 13. Social Media Intelligence

Recommendations may include continue, improve, pause, retire, repurpose, or scale. They require enough verified data and must not invent best times or optimize vanity metrics without business relevance.

Publishing, credentials, Ads, billing, and spend remain separately gated.

## 15. Knowledge Base

The Knowledge Base preserves project decisions, PRs, issues, evidence, designs, rights, QA findings, content, SEO, publishing receipts, training knowledge, adaptive strategies, templates, agent reports, incidents, and lessons learned.

Records have a source, date, owner, sensitivity, approval status, version, review date, and lifecycle state: draft, reviewed, approved, superseded, or archived.

Credentials are never stored. Superseded guidance remains traceable but is not presented as current.

## 16. Owner Dashboard and Notifications

The owner view reports evidence-backed project progress, agents, tasks, blockers, decisions, quality gates, readiness, system health, and future verified business metrics.

Notifications include agent completion/blocking, QA results, PR/CI state, decision needs, missing evidence, publishing readiness, expired evidence, security/reliability warnings, and weekly/monthly reports.

In-app notifications come first. Email, WhatsApp, or other outbound delivery is separately gated. Every alert links to evidence and an exact action. Invented percentages, revenue, leads, bookings, or completion claims are prohibited.

## 18. Director challenge rule

The project director must challenge, defer, or reject proposals that introduce:

- unsupported medical, therapeutic, rehabilitation, qualification, or outcome claims;
- unsafe swimming instruction;
- privacy, safeguarding, credential, or security exposure;
- duplicated systems of record;
- premature complexity that delays higher-value foundations;
- unbounded operating cost or paid spend;
- destructive or unapproved Production impact;
- conflict with Revenue-First sequencing or approved factual boundaries.

The director records the conflict and provides a safer alternative instead of agreeing automatically.

## 19. Protected approvals

Routine, reversible, evidence-backed internal work proceeds under Issue #70.

Dedicated approval or qualified input remains required for:

- budget, billing, paid spend, and stop-loss values;
- credentials and external write access;
- legal entity, controller, privacy facts, and legal publication acceptance;
- real customer, lead, child, family, disability, diagnosis, health, or safeguarding data;
- Production migration, destructive write, real booking, or outbound communication;
- public medical, therapy, rehabilitation, certification, or outcome claims;
- clinician-supervised assessment or treatment decisions.
