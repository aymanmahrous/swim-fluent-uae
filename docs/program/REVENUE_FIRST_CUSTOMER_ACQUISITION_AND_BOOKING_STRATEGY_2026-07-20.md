# Revenue-First Customer Acquisition and Booking Strategy

## Commercial objective

Generate qualified initial-assessment requests, then convert them into confirmed appointments only after a real location-calendar conflict check and human review where needed.

## Priority audiences

1. Parents seeking learn-to-swim, water confidence or calmer small-group coaching for children.
2. Families with two or more children who benefit from the approved sibling rate.
3. People interested in non-medical aquatic or land-based movement sessions.
4. Searchers near Al Najda, Al Falah, Khalifa, Mushrif and Al Danah.

## Value proposition

- Maximum four children in a group.
- Clear initial assessment before confirmation.
- Five selectable Abu Dhabi locations.
- Weekday and weekend general availability.
- Approved sibling rate.
- Direct WhatsApp path with no automatic message sending.

## Conversion path

1. Organic post, search result, referral or future approved advertisement.
2. Localized landing page.
3. Pricing and nearest-location card.
4. “Request an initial assessment.”
5. Service, location and requested time.
6. General-hours validation.
7. Location-specific Google Calendar free/busy check.
8. Internal notification to `relaxfix2026@gmail.com`.
9. Human review if conflict, special question or unclear request.
10. Confirmed event with safe reference-only title.
11. Transactional confirmation and reminder only.
12. Post-session follow-up only with customer consent.

## Channel order

1. Website conversion foundation and Local SEO.
2. Organic Instagram/Meta pilot after rights, consent and publishing gates.
3. Google Search Ads only after real conversion proof, approved budget and stop-loss.
4. Meta Ads only after organic and Google learning.

## First 30 days

### Week 1 — conversion foundation

- Approve public prices, location names, WhatsApp and operational email.
- Validate mobile Arabic/English pages and all Maps links.
- Establish one calendar per location or an equivalent resource-calendar model.
- Run fictional Calendar/email/n8n tests with external writes disabled.

### Week 2 — local intent

- Prepare one bilingual page/section per verified training area.
- Complete GBP and Search Console read-only evidence.
- Publish no claim, profile change or post until the release gate is approved.
- Prepare location-specific organic posts with exact Maps destination.

### Week 3 — organic pilot

- Release the smallest approved asset/copy set.
- Route all CTAs to the initial-assessment flow.
- Measure no-PII events: location CTA, WhatsApp click, conversation start and completed request.
- Review response time, qualified-request rate and slot-conflict rate.

### Week 4 — optimize before paid media

- Keep the best location/value proposition combinations.
- Fix mobile, availability and response-time friction.
- Prepare a limited Google Search campaign pack.
- Do not launch spend until conversion proof, billing owner, currency, ceiling and stop-loss are approved.

## Operational metrics

- Qualified initial-assessment requests per week.
- Request-to-confirmed-appointment rate.
- Median human response time.
- Calendar conflict rate.
- Duplicate-prevention count.
- Location demand by requested site.
- WhatsApp CTA to request-completion rate.
- Cancellation/reschedule rate.

No metric may contain names, phone numbers, child data, health details or free-text messages.

## Stop conditions

- A slot is shown without a location-specific conflict check.
- Duplicate or ambiguous Calendar response.
- Missing consent for contact/follow-up.
- Medical or rehabilitation claim without approval.
- PII appears in analytics, workflow logs or event titles.
- External send occurs while test mode is enabled.
- Production environment or credentials cannot be proven.

## Owner decision queue

1. Approve neutral public wording for the AED 150 aquatic/land-based sessions, or provide regulatory evidence for rehabilitation wording.
2. Choose the Calendar ownership/resource model for five locations and authorize OAuth for `relaxfix2026@gmail.com`.
3. Confirm location-specific availability and travel buffers.
4. Provide n8n workspace access and credential custody; keep the workflow inactive until test evidence passes.
5. Approve the exact transactional sender/provider and reply-to policy.
6. Approve a later Production release candidate after Preview, accessibility and end-to-end fictional tests pass.
